## Anthropic's Two-Front War: Ethics vs the Pentagon

The biggest Anthropic news this week wasn't technology — it was politics.

The Pentagon officially designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the two sides couldn't agree on AI model control — **the military wanted unrestricted use of Claude, including for autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance. Anthropic refused. The $200M contract collapsed.**

> AI models are rapidly commoditizing. Anthropic is positioning itself as the "ethical and trustworthy AI provider" — and that has real market value for both consumers and enterprises. — Bruce Schneier & Nathan Sanders

Ironically, **Claude's consumer growth actually accelerated**. TechCrunch reports Claude App new installs have surpassed ChatGPT, with Anthropic's annual revenue reaching **$19B ARR**.

Meanwhile, OpenAI's "compromise" placed Anthropic in a more complex position: MIT Technology Review reported OpenAI accepted some Pentagon terms — precisely the precedent Anthropic feared.

## Proton Mail Privacy Incident: Encryption Doesn't Mean Anonymity

A widely discussed case: **Proton Mail assisted the FBI in unmasking an anonymous "Stop Cop City" protester**.

The core logic: Proton Mail encrypted email content, but IP addresses and backup recovery emails were not in scope. Under legal demands, Proton provided this "metadata."

**This is a reminder: threat models matter. Encrypted content does not equal complete anonymity.**

## AI Engineering Anti-Patterns: Simon Willison's Warnings

Simon Willison organized the most common developer mistakes in his "Agentic Engineering Patterns" guide:

- **Inflicting unreviewed code on collaborators**: Don't submit PRs you haven't read yourself
- **Over-trusting AI test results**: Agents can execute code, but that doesn't mean they understand what the test is testing
- **No Prompt Injection protection**: Any agent receiving user input must be defended
- **Sharing cache keys across workflows**: The Cline case lesson (see W04)

## BlackRock $26B Private Credit Fund Limits Redemptions

A notable financial market signal this week: **BlackRock limited redemptions on its $26B private credit fund** — client withdrawal requests surged to 9.3% of NAV.
