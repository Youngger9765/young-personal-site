## OpenAI Closes the Largest Startup Raise in History: $110B

OpenAI closed a round led by Amazon, NVIDIA, and SoftBank totaling **$110 billion** at a **$840B valuation** — the largest startup fundraise in history.

> "Congratulations, you secured the biggest number." — Latent Space

This capital gives OpenAI resources to build AI infrastructure for years to come. Competitor Anthropic's annual revenue has reached $19B ARR, and the gap between the two AI giants is narrowing.

## Cursor Announces the "Third Era": Cloud Agents Overtake IDE Completion

$50B-valued Cursor announced this week that **cloud agent usage has surpassed traditional IDE tab completion**. They call it the "Third Era of Software Development."

Three pillars:

1. **Agents test their own code**: Models don't just write — they run tests, iterate, and deliver verified PRs
2. **Video playback**: Agents record a video of their session, far more intuitive than reading diffs
3. **Full remote control**: Developers can take over the VM directly — mouse clicks, keyboard input

Cursor also acquired Graphite (Git tools) and Autotab (Computer Use pioneer).

> The next breakthrough is "widening the pipe" — parallel agents, agent swarms — not one person with one model. — Jonas (Cursor co-founder)

## Mitchell Hashimoto: How AI Reshapes Individual Engineering Work

HashiCorp founder Mitchell Hashimoto shared how AI agents have fundamentally changed his daily engineering workflow:

- No more "how to start" friction — AI is the best rubber duck
- Agents can explore codebases, find relevant docs, and run tests, freeing him to focus on architecture decisions
- He prefers agents that **act autonomously** over Copilot-style "suggestions"

The Pragmatic Engineer simultaneously published "Six Predictions for AI in Software Engineering," distilled from the Pragmatic Summit workshop: AI is reshaping not just tools, but the entire engineering role.

## Vercel and the Frontend Ecosystem

Vercel's blog remained the highest-volume source this week (151 articles), though beginning to plateau vs. W01. Key highlights:

- **Streamdown 2.4**: More customization hooks, accessibility features, Markdown rendering improvements
- **Run cron jobs from deployment summary**: Launch scheduled tasks directly post-deploy
- **v0 private Blob Store**: Conversational UI for managing storage

The ai-sdk continues to be the top choice for frontend developers integrating AI, driving substantial technical discussion.

## Google Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite: Ultra-Low-Cost Inference

Google released **Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite**, priced at $0.25/M input and $1.5/M output — **1/8 the price of Gemini 3.1 Pro**. It supports four thinking levels (minimal / low / medium / high) and is among the most cost-efficient multimodal models on the market.

Simon Willison tested all four thinking levels and shared "four pelicans at different depths of thought."
