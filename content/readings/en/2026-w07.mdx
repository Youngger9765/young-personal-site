## Tech Jobs: Worse Than the 2008 or 2020 Recessions

One of the most-discussed technical topics on Hacker News this week: **tech employment is now significantly worse than either the 2008 or 2020 recessions**.

Key data (from job market analysis):
- Tech job openings declined more than during either comparison recession period
- AI adoption is accelerating some position reductions
- Block (formerly Square) announced **40% layoffs**, officially stating "not due to AI" — but the timing is telling
- Large company "efficiency" drives meet AI acceleration amid macro uncertainty

> Anthropic's research "Labor market impacts of AI: A new measure and early evidence" provides early quantitative evidence — AI is genuinely changing labor market structure, not just improving productivity.

## The Language After Kotlin: JetBrains' Next Move

The Pragmatic Engineer deeply interviewed Kotlin's creator about what the next programming language will be:

- Kotlin's success came from **pragmatism**: solving Java's pain points rather than chasing theoretical purity
- The next generation language needs native AI collaboration support: type systems that let LLMs better understand and generate code
- **Kotlin Multiplatform** (KMP) is Kotlin's next big bet

## AI Enables Anyone to Write CUDA Kernels

HuggingFace Blog published an exciting piece: **how Codex and Claude enable anyone to write custom CUDA kernels**.

Previously, writing CUDA required deep GPU architecture knowledge — a rare skill. Now:
- AI can generate efficient CUDA cores from natural language descriptions
- This lets more researchers do GPU-level optimization without being limited to PyTorch APIs
- HuggingFace demonstrated several cases: 2-5x training speedups, kernels entirely AI-generated

## Kent Beck's Engineering Generational Philosophy

Kent Beck tackled a sensitive topic this week:

**"Generation Gap or Just Rude?"**:
- The divergence between young engineers' and senior engineers' attitudes toward AI
- Not an age issue, but different **tolerance for uncertainty**
- Senior engineers' advantage: ability to recognize when AI output is wrong

**"Genie Session: Codex for Mac"**:
- Kent's personal experiment: using AI Genie to help write a Mac app
- Finding: AI's strength isn't new features — it's "accelerating things you already know how to do"
